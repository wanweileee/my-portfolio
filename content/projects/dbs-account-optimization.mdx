## The brief

Onboarding for a banking product means moving documents, identities, and verification steps through a system that has to stay correct on every step. The brief was to build an account opening flow that was both fast for the user and rigorous behind the scenes.

## What I made

- A Ruby on Rails app developed with **TDD/BDD** from day one — every feature shipped with a behavioural spec describing what it was supposed to do for whom.
- **OCR-based document extraction** that lifts structured customer data off uploaded IDs and supporting documents, with manual review as a safety net.
- **OTP microservices** wired into the flow at the verification gates, so identity checks are isolated, testable, and replaceable.
- Automated validation and verification pipelines that catch malformed data before it reaches a human reviewer.

## What I learned

Writing the spec before the code is annoying for the first two weeks and a gift for the next six months. The places where the spec was hardest to write were almost always the places where the requirements were unclear — TDD turned out to be a requirements-discovery tool as much as a testing one.
