## The brief

Road safety teams have plenty of after-the-fact data on accidents but very little predictive signal on *where* the next ones are likely to happen. The project's question: can a model identify hotspot road segments early enough for intervention?

## What I made

- Predictive models built on a fused dataset of **geospatial road network features**, **sensor and traffic data**, **environmental conditions**, and **time-series accident history**.
- Structured feature engineering with explicit emphasis on interpretability — every input feature has a story a road-safety stakeholder can follow.
- Evaluation using metrics appropriate for *imbalanced spatial classification* (not just accuracy), with calibration plots and confidence intervals on hotspot rankings.
- Dashboards and structured graphs that surface hotspot risk scores in a way decision-makers can act on without reading the modelling.

## What I learned

The hardest part of a "predict the rare bad event" model is not the model — it's getting non-technical stakeholders to trust a probabilistic ranking. Visualisations that show *why* a segment is flagged moved the needle on adoption far more than another point of AUC.
