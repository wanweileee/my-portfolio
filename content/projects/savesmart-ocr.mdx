## The brief

Most people don't track their spending because the *input* is painful: typing every line item, picking a category, repeating tomorrow. SaveSmart eats the receipt and does the categorising itself.

## What I made

- A **React + Flask** full-stack app where users snap a receipt and the system handles the rest.
- An **OCR pipeline** that extracts raw text, then cleans and structures the receipt data into normalised line items with merchant, amount, and date.
- **NLP-based classification** that assigns each line item to a spending category automatically — and learns from the corrections a user makes.
- Analytics views that surface spending patterns without making them feel like an accusation.

## What I learned

The data was always messier than the demos suggested. The line between "good enough OCR" and "actually usable" was small, and the difference was almost entirely in the cleaning step — not the model.

> The model is a junior employee. The pipeline around it is the manager.
