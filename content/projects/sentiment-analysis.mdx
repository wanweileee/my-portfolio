## The brief

Product decisions get made on *what people say* about a product, but the saying happens scattered across YouTube comments, Amazon reviews, and Reddit threads. The brief: build a pipeline that turns that scatter into something a product team can actually use.

## What I made

- **Data collection** at scale from YouTube, Amazon, and Reddit using APIs and web scraping where APIs ran out.
- **NLP sentiment models** that classify consumer opinions and surface the themes underneath them — not just positive/negative but *why*.
- A translation layer that converts unstructured comment text into **structured product signals** (feature requests, complaints, comparisons) a PM can rank against.

## What I learned

Sentiment alone is a fairly thin signal. The interesting work was extracting *what the sentiment is about* — the model that says "this review is angry" matters less than the one that says "this review is angry about the battery." That second model is where decisions get made.
